🧴 What everyday things can I claim? (German rules)

A cost is deductible only if it is caused by the rental AND a cost you actually bear. Repairs and upkeep are "Erhaltungsaufwand", deducted in full in the year you pay. §9 EStG ↗

Everyday itemClaim?Why
Schimmelspray, cleaning & small-repair materials used in the flatYesLandlord-side cleaning/repair to keep it lettable = Erhaltungsaufwand, full deduction that year
Paint, sealant, fittings, minor repairsYesRepair material for the let
Rauchmelder (smoke detectors), small appliancesYesA unit under 800 € net writes off immediately (GWG)
Toilet paper, soap, in-flat bulbs during tenancy (unfurnished)NoThe tenant lives there and buys their own consumables, not your cost
The same, in a furnished / holiday let you supplyYesWhen you provide them as part of a furnished let
Examples: Schimmelspray = yes (used on the rented flat); toilet paper = no for a normal unfurnished let, that is the tenant's own cost. Keep the receipt either way.

Recent & leading court rulings

BFH IX R 19/24 (14.01.2025)WEG maintenance reserve is deductible only when actually spent. ↗
BFH IX R 26/19 (2020)A contract's land/building split binds unless economically untenable. ↗
BFH IX R 25/14 (2016)Even cosmetic Schönheitsreparaturen count toward the 15% 3-year limit. ↗
Pitfalls: (1) the 15% trap in the first 3 years (below). (2) Spread big repair bills over 2 to 5 years (§82b EStDV). (3) Items under 800 € net write off immediately. (4) Pay non-cash, keep receipts made out to you. (5) Don't pad the return with tenant consumables.

General guidance, not individual advice; confirm with your Steuerberater.

🇩🇪 The 10-year tax-free sale (Germany)

A privately-held rental sold more than 10 years after purchase has a tax-free gain (§23 EStG). The clock starts at the notary purchase date. §23 EStG ↗
  • Sell just past the 10-year line and past the fixed-rate end, so the whole gain is tax-free and there is no early-repayment penalty.
  • Sell within 10 years instead and the gain is taxed at your marginal rate, with claimed depreciation added back.
  • One property sold once = private. Avoid selling more than 3 in 5 years.
  • ⚠️ Confirm the exact tax-free date with your Steuerberater before signing.

⚡ How overpaying works

Think of an overpayment as a savings account paying your mortgage rate, except the money lowers your loan instead of sitting in an account. You stop paying interest on the part you cleared.

1
Every 1.000 € you overpay saves your interest rate on that amount, every year, until the loan is gone.
2
It compounds: a smaller balance means less interest next year too.
3
After tax the benefit is smaller, because mortgage interest is tax-deductible; you give up a little deduction. Still a guaranteed, risk-free return.
